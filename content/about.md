Eric Idle (born 29 March 1943) is an English actor, comedian, author, singer, songwriter, musician, and playwright. He is a member of the British surreal comedy group Monty Python and the author of their Broadway musical *Spamalot*.

## Early Life

Born in South Shields, County Durham, Idle was raised in relative poverty following the death of his father in a hitchhiking accident on Christmas Eve 1945. He was enrolled at the Royal Wolverhampton School as a boarder at the age of seven, where the strict regime — including being woken at dawn by a bell, attending chapel before breakfast, and enduring cold showers — prepared him admirably for a career in comedy.

## Monty Python

Idle joined Monty Python in 1969 alongside John Cleese, Terry Gilliam, Terry Jones, Michael Palin, and Graham Chapman. He is credited with writing the song "Always Look on the Bright Side of Life," originally performed in *Monty Python's Life of Brian* (1979) and later adopted as a genuine funeral favourite across Britain.

He was often referred to as "the sixth Python" by people who could not count, and as "the third tallest Python" by people who could.

## Philosophy

Idle has described his working method as follows: "I write when I'm inspired, and I see to it that I'm inspired at nine o'clock every morning." He is a firm believer that the best comedy emerges from a disciplined approach to absurdity — not unlike software consulting, in that regard.

## Notable Achievements

- Co-wrote and starred in *Monty Python and the Holy Grail* (1975)
- Wrote *Spamalot*, which won the Tony Award for Best Musical (2005)
- Sang "Always Look on the Bright Side of Life" at the closing ceremony of the 2012 London Olympics
- Has never, to anyone's knowledge, successfully operated a modern build pipeline
